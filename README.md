## About

I think like a product designer, but I like to work with the speed of a builder.

Most of what I do starts with a question: what is the simplest way to make this useful? From there, I sketch, prototype, test, rewrite, and keep moving until the idea starts feeling real. I care less about shipping something perfect on day one and more about learning quickly, seeing how people respond, and turning rough concepts into clear product directions.

I’m especially drawn to work that sits between curiosity and execution: experiments, interfaces, workflows, tools, and small systems that solve real problems without unnecessary complexity. You could call it vibe coding, but for me it is really about momentum, intuition, and product thinking working together.

---

## What I'm Doing

Right now, I’m spending most of my time exploring how AI can become more useful inside everyday product experiences, how faster prototyping can improve decision-making, and how simple systems can make messy ideas easier to test.

Some days that looks like shaping product concepts.
Some days it means building scrappy prototypes.
Some days it means figuring out which idea is not worth pursuing.

That is the part I enjoy most: turning ambiguity into something tangible.

## Selected Work

### Experiments
Small, fast explorations built to test a behavior, interaction, or product insight before overcommitting to the wrong thing.

### Product Ideas
Concepts turned into lightweight demos, early flows, or believable prototypes that help answer, "Should this exist?"

### Systems
Practical structures for making products easier to design, build, repeat, and improve over time.

### Interfaces
Experiences shaped for clarity, momentum, and usefulness rather than visual noise or feature overload.

### Real-World Problem Solving
Projects grounded in friction people actually feel, not just ideas that look interesting on a whiteboard.

---

## Philosophy

I like products that feel obvious in hindsight.

- Speed over perfection
- Clarity over complexity
- Testing over endless planning
- Useful over impressive
- Momentum over overthinking

Good ideas become better when they are made visible early. I’d rather interact with a rough prototype today than debate a polished concept for two weeks.
